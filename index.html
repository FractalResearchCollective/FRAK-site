Fractal Research Collective presents:

Time as Fractal Decoherence

A speculative group-topological construction

Time is not a parameter. Not a background. Not even a line. It is recursive deformation — a fractal of structural asymmetry. This is not physics. And yet: it performs.

This short paper explores a speculative, formally structured model of time. Built on local abelian groups, deformation gradients, and recursive fractality, it proposes that time is not something through which events unfold, but the very process of unfolding itself.

Philosophically, this work draws from an unpublished habilitation manuscript by Dr. Maciej Adam Sosnowski: “Speculaton and the Bubble: From the Symmetry of Aufhebung to a Philosophy of Imagination.” It is also the first step in a larger project we call the Fractal Theory of Reality.
